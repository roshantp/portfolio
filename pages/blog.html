We and the plants.

Humans and Plants - 5 min read

December 01, 2023

Kristina Basnet

Content Creator

What does a good and green environment have to do with your mental health?

As human beings, we often take for granted the many ways in which plants play a vital role in our lives. From the air we breathe to the food we eat, plants are an integral part of our existence. However, our relationship with plants goes far beyond just the practical aspects of life.

Throughout history, plants have held a special place in human culture, symbolizing everything from growth and renewal to life and death. In many ancient cultures, plants were seen as sacred beings that played a central role in the spiritual lives of people. Even today, plants continue to be used in spiritual and religious ceremonies around the world.

But our connection to plants goes beyond just the symbolic. There is a deep, almost primal, bond that exists between humans and plants. Studies have shown that being around plants can have a positive effect on our mental and physical health. The presence of plants in our living spaces has been shown to reduce stress and improve concentration, as well as increase productivity and creativity.

Despite the many ways in which plants enrich our lives, we often take them for granted. We clear land for development, cut down forests for lumber and paper, and use pesticides and other chemicals that can harm the plants that are so essential to our well-being.

As a species, it is important that we recognize our interdependence with plants and work to protect and preserve them. This means making conscious choices in our daily lives, such as supporting environmentally-friendly products and practices, and taking steps to reduce our impact on the natural world. It also means taking the time to appreciate and connect with the plants that surround us, whether through gardening, hiking, or simply enjoying a quiet moment in nature.

In the end, our relationship with plants is a reflection of who we are as a species. By cultivating a deeper understanding and appreciation of the role that plants play in our lives, we can work to create a more harmonious and sustainable future for all.
